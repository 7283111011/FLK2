The intestacy rules

Section 46 Of The Administration Of Estates Act 1925

Section 46 of the Administration of Estates Act 1925 essentially lays out the order of relatives who inherit when someone dies without a will, which is called dying intestate. The section says that first, the spouse or civil partner will inherit a substantial part of the estate, and if there are no children, they may inherit everything. If there are children, then the estate is divided between the spouse and the children. If there is no spouse, then it goes down the line to children, then to other relatives like parents, siblings, and so on.

Examination focus: If you get a question about intestacy rules and specifically section 46 of the Administration of Estates Act, what you want to focus on is the statutory order of who inherits when someone dies without a will. In an examination scenario, you will need to know the hierarchy of relatives who are entitled to inherit under intestacy. that usually starts with the spouse or civil partner, then children, and then other relatives if there are no closer heirs. The key point is to understand who inherits and in what order if there is no will. If you see a question on that, think about the intestacy hierarchy and who is entitled to inherit.
